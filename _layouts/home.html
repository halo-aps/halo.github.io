Project

Our project aims to cultivate scientific curiosity among the Latinx community and empower its members to take ownership of their scientific experiences.

We are opening CU's Observatory and Planetarium to the local Latinx communities, by providing programs in their native language, and creating mobile programs with portable telescopes to reach them in their own environments.

.

Break Barriers

We aim to eliminate barriers that have historically prevented the Latinx community from engaging with CU spaces. These include limited exposure, language barriers, and a lack of sense of belonging.

Educational Content

We co-create educational content in Spanish for CU's facilities, tailored to the needs of the Latinx communities in Boulder.

Representation

We aspire to lead by example. By demonstrating it is possible to achieve the highest levels of scientific education, we aim to show that there is room for Latinxs in academic environments.

Training Sessions

We host training sessions for Spanish speakers, where they learn to operate the 20-inch telescopes at the Observatory, a portable 8-inch telescope, and a portable H-alpha 40mm solar telescope.

About

This is our story...

  • 2021-2022

    Multiculturalism at the Observatory (MO)

    We were awarded a Micro Grant by CU's Office for Outreach and Engagement and we launched our first outreach project. MO was a bilingual astronomy outreach program that enabled community members to explore the cosmos and the connection between astronomy and hispanic cultures. The program hosted alternating English and Spanish online observing nights at CU's Sommers-Bausch Observatory.

  • 2023-2024

    Hispanic Outreach at Fiske and the Observatory (HOFO)

    We won an Outreach Award by CU's Office for Outreach and Engagement. We officially partered with the Sommers-Bausch Observatory, the Fiske Planetarium and Explorando Senderos, and took our project one step further: opening CU's spaces for the Latinx families. Our program included science talks in Spanish followed by scientific demonstrations led by the community, observing nights and training in operating a portable telescope.

    Hispanic Astronomy for Learning and Observation (HALO)
